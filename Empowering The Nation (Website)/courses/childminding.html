Child Minding

Build a strong foundation in caring for infants and toddlers with practical, confidence-building techniques you can use immediately in a home environment. This six-week programme focuses on safety, nurturing routines, and early learning through play.

Six-week programme Fee: R750

Who this is for:

  • Domestic workers and caregivers supporting families with infants or toddlers.
  • Helpers transitioning into childcare roles at home.
  • Anyone seeking essential skills in child safety, nutrition, and development.

What you’ll gain:

  • Daily care routines that keep children safe, calm, and healthy.
  • Age-appropriate play and stimulation to support early development.
  • Clear communication with parents, including routines and feedback.

Course Modules

Birth to six-month needs Safety · Feeding · Soothing Expand Collapse
  • Newborn safety: safe sleep, temperature checks, hygiene basics.
  • Feeding routines and cues (breastmilk/formula handling, burping).
  • Soothing techniques: swaddling, holding, comforting strategies.
  • When to alert parents and how to log daily care notes.
Seven-month to one-year needs Solids · Motor skills · Routines Expand Collapse
  • Introducing solids safely, textures, and basic allergy awareness.
  • Motor skills: supported sitting, crawling, assisted standing.
  • Play ideas: cause-and-effect toys, sensory baskets, early books.
  • Daily rhythms: naps, feeding windows, bath and bedtime flow.
Toddler Needs (1–3 years) Positive discipline · Learning through play Expand Collapse
  • Positive discipline: boundaries, consistency, praise, redirection.
  • Language, movement, and social play for growing confidence.
  • Nutrition, hydration, and safe snacking on active days.
  • Hazard awareness: home safety at toddler level.
Educational toys & Early learning Low-cost activities · Reading · Music Expand Collapse
  • Choosing age-appropriate toys for different stages.
  • Simple, low-cost activities using household items.
  • Reading time and music for attention, memory, and joy.
  • Setting up a calm, inclusive play space at home.
