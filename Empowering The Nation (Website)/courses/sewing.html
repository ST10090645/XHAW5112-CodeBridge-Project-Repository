Sewing

Master essential machine and hand-sewing techniques for household repairs, simple garments, and alterations. This six-month learnership builds job-ready confidence.

Six-month learnership Fee: R1500

Who this is for:

  • Domestic workers managing linens and clothing repairs.
  • Beginners aiming to sew and alter basic garments.
  • Anyone seeking a practical, income-earning skill.

What you’ll gain:

  • Machine setup, threading, tension, and maintenance.
  • Core seams, hems, zips, and buttonholes.
  • Pattern reading, measuring, and fitting basics.

Course Modules

Machine Fundamentals Threading · Tension · Maintenance ExpandCollapse
  • Parts, needles, bobbins, and accessories.
  • Threading steps and common fixes.
  • Cleaning, oiling, and storage.
Stitches & Finishes Seams · Hems · Zippers ExpandCollapse
  • Plain, French, and zigzag seams.
  • Hemming methods and edge finishes.
  • Installing zippers and buttonholes.
Patterns & Fitting Measuring · Cutting · Adjustments ExpandCollapse
  • Reading pattern symbols and grainlines.
  • Cutting layouts and fabric handling.
  • Simple alterations and fitting tips.
Repairs & Upcycling Patching · Reinforcing · Reuse ExpandCollapse
  • Repairing seams, holes, and tears.
  • Reinforcing stress points.
  • Creative reuse of textiles.
